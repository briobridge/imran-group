Our Heritage

Half a Century of Honourable Enterprise

"The Imran Group did not emerge overnight. It was quarried — slowly, deliberately — from the bedrock of Lahori commerce, shaped by decades of discipline, and polished by the exacting standards of a family for whom reputation is the only currency that truly endures."

The First Venture

In 1972, a young Muhammad Imran Ahmed — just returned from London with a degree in economics and an unshakeable belief in Pakistan's commercial potential — registered Imran Trading Co. in a modest office on The Mall. With a single telephone, a ledger, and a network of contacts cultivated through sheer force of character, he began importing industrial textiles and exporting finished goods to the Gulf States. Within three years, Imran Trading Co. had become one of Lahore's most reliable commodity houses.

1972
1981

Into Property & Land

Having accumulated both capital and wisdom, Mr. Ahmed pivoted toward real estate — a sector he had long identified as the true measure of a city's ambition. Imran Properties was founded in 1981, initially acquiring and developing commercial land in Gulberg and Garden Town. The timing proved prescient: Lahore's urban expansion through the 1980s transformed modest land holdings into some of the city's most coveted commercial addresses, establishing the family fortune on foundations that have never wavered.

Hospitality & Refinement

By the mid-1990s, the Imran Group had earned sufficient stature to enter the hospitality sector. Crescent Hospitality was established in 1995 with the opening of a boutique hotel in Gulberg — a property celebrated for its restrained elegance and impeccable service. Mr. Ahmed held that luxury was not a matter of ostentation but of care: the right glass, the correct temperature, the unhurried attention of staff who understood that a guest's comfort was a matter of honour. Crescent set a standard that competitors have spent three decades attempting to match.

1995
2003

The Digital Age

When Mr. Ahmed's son, Zubair Imran, identified the nascent technology retail market of Pakistan in the early 2000s, his father did not hesitate. Laptop King opened its first showroom in Lahore's MM Alam Road in 2003 — a meticulously designed space that brought premium computing to a discerning Pakistani clientele. What began as a single shop has evolved into the nation's foremost authority on premium technology, with a clientele that includes corporations, government institutions, and the country's most distinguished professionals.

Capital & Stewardship

The establishment of Gulberg Capital in 2010 marked the Group's maturation into a full-fledged financial institution. Where once the family managed its own wealth through instinct and conservatism, Gulberg Capital now extends those same principles — prudence, patience, long-horizon thinking — to a select clientele of high-net-worth individuals and institutional investors. The firm manages assets across equities, real estate investment trusts, and private equity, guided always by the founding philosophy: preserve first, then grow.

Leadership & Vision

The Man Behind the Empire

Muhammad Imran Ahmed · Founder & Chairman · Est. 1972
MI

Muhammad
Imran Ahmed

Founder & Chairman
b. 1948 · Lahore

"I have built nothing that I was not prepared to sign my name to. Every building, every partnership, every company bears my commitment — and I do not make commitments lightly."

Born in 1948 to a family of modest merchants in the old quarters of Lahore, Muhammad Imran Ahmed was raised on the twin gospels of hard work and honest dealing. His father, a textile trader, instilled in the young Imran a reverence for the handshake — not as a legal instrument, but as a sacred bond between honourable men.

Educated first at Aitchison College, Lahore — where he excelled in mathematics and debate — he proceeded to the London School of Economics, graduating in 1970 with a degree in Economics. The years in England were formative in ways beyond the academic: exposure to the great British trading houses, the City's investment culture, and the quiet confidence of established wealth gave him a model he would spend his life quietly, purposefully replicating on Pakistani soil.

He returned to Lahore in 1971, during one of Pakistan's most turbulent periods. Where others saw uncertainty, Imran Ahmed saw opportunity — the opportunity to build something durable precisely when durability was most scarce. In 1972, with a borrowed office on The Mall and a capital base assembled through family connections and personal savings, he registered Imran Trading Co. — the seed from which a conglomerate would grow.

The 1970s taught him the virtue of patience. The 1980s, the discipline of diversification. The 1990s, the rewards of reputation. By the 2000s, Imran Ahmed was no longer merely a businessman — he was an institution. His counsel was sought by ministers and industrialists alike. His signature on a contract was sufficient guarantee for any counterparty. His name on a building told prospective tenants everything they needed to know.

Now in his seventy-sixth year, Mr. Ahmed remains actively engaged in the Group's strategic direction — attending board meetings, meeting key partners, and insisting, as he always has, that the standard of everything bearing the Imran name must be beyond reproach. He is assisted by his son, Zubair Imran, who serves as Group Managing Director, and his daughter, Sana Imran Malik, who oversees Gulberg Capital with the same methodical precision as her father.

He lives with his wife, Begum Nusrat Ahmed, in the same house in Gulberg III that he purchased in 1978 — a fact he mentions with quiet satisfaction whenever anyone suggests he might prefer something more modern.
